Produced by Sudhakar Mikkilineni and Kosaraju Harikrishna under the banners Yuvasudha Arts and N. T. R. Arts, the film stars N. T. Rama Rao Jr. in the titular role, alongside Saif Ali Khan and Janhvi Kapoor, both making their Telugu debuts, alongside an ensemble of Prakash Raj, Srikanth, Shine Tom Chacko, and Narain.

It is the first part of a two-part film. The film was officially announced in April 2021 under the tentative title NTR 30, as it is Rama Rao's 30th film in the leading role, while the official title was announced in May 2023. Principal photography commenced in April 2023 in Hyderabad followed with a schedule in Goa. The film has music composed by Anirudh Ravichander, cinematography handled by R. Rathnavelu and editing by A. Sreekar Prasad.

Production

Development

In April 2021, it was reported that Rama Rao and Siva would collaborate for NTR30, marking their second collaboration after Janatha Garage (2016).[5] The film was announcement on Ugadi (13 April 2021) with Yuvasudha Arts producing the film in collaboration with N. T. R. Arts.[6] However, Siva's commitments on Acharya and Rama Rao's on RRR had delayed the start of production.

Siva described it as a "large-scale project that has more commercial appeal" in comparison to his debut film Mirchi (2013).[8] Following the box-office failure of Acharya, Siva wanted to fine-tune the script making necessary changes before finalizing it as a draft, which meant that the commencement of filming has been delayed throughout late 2022.[9][10] By November, Siva started the pre-production works of the film with production set to begin in early 2023.

Initially set to begin production on 24 February 2023, the commencement was delayed due to the demise of Taraka Ratna as well as Rama Rao's participation in the Academy Awards campaign for RRR.[12] The film was officially launched on 23 March 2023 with a puja ceremony held in Hyderabad with the presence of the cast and crew and S. S. Rajamouli preceding with the muhurat shot.[13] The film's title Devara was officially announced on 19 May 2023.[14] On 4 October, Siva announced that the film would be split into two parts.


Casting

The technical crew consist of musician Anirudh Ravichander, cinematographer R. Rathnavelu, editor A. Sreekar Prasad, production designer Sabu Cyril, stunt choreographer Kenny Bates and visual effects supervisors Brad Minnich and Yugandhar T.[16]

Alia Bhatt, Shraddha Kapoor, Mrunal Thakur and Rashmika Mandanna were considered to play the female lead, but turned down the offer.[17][18] Janhvi Kapoor was confirmed to play lead actress, in her South Indian film debut.[19] At the India Today Conclave, Janhvi described it as a "dream come true" moment on working with Rama Rao as she recalled on how she manifested her debut in South industry through Rama Rao's film in her past interviews.[20] In February 2023, Vikram, Vijay Sethupathi and Saif Ali Khan were reported to play the antagonists.[21] Since the former two denied their roles, Khan was finalized as a part of the cast.[3]

Prakash Raj and Meera Jasmine participated at the launch ceremony, thereby being part of the film.[22] In May 2023, further supporting cast members joined the film with Mani Chandana and Tarak Ponnappa being part of the sets.[23][24] In June 2023, it was announced that Shine Tom Chacko was cast in the film.[4] Kalaiyarasan, Narain, Murali Sharma and Abhimanyu Singh also joined the cast after filming few prelimnary sequences.[


Filming

Principal photography commenced on 1 April 2023 in Hyderabad, with Rama Rao in attendance,[27] while Janhvi and Khan joined the sets in mid April.[28][3] In mid-May, the team took a two-week break following Rama Rao's departure for a family vacation.[29] Filming resumed on 7 June 2023 with few crucial sequences between Rama Rao, Khan and other cast members being shot there and was completed 14 days.[30][31] Several action sequences had been shot in this schedule, including one which has been filmed in extreme low light.[32] The team then took a brief month-long break, before resuming on 31 July 2023, to shoot an underwater action sequence.[33] Rama Rao trained with expert swimmers from Mumbai for this particular schedule.[34] In September, some of the sequences have been shot at a specially constructed beach set in Shamshabad.[35]

On 4 October 2023, the makers filmed the underwater action sequence at Hyderabad which would conclude the schedule post-completion.[36] The team then moved to Goa, for filming the concurrent schedule on Dusshera (24 October 2023).[37] Janhvi completed filming for her portions on 1 November.[38] The team then took a break to celebrate Diwali (12 November 2023) and resumed filming two days later for another brief schedule.[39] The Goa schedule was completed within 20 November and the team returned to Hyderabad for another brief schedule which was filmed during mid-December.[40] Kalyan Ram revealed that 80% of the film's shooting had been completed by late-December 2023, and the filming would be wrapped by January 2024.
